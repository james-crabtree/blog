GROUP PROJECT REFLECTIONS: My three roles

Participating in group projects in weeks 2-6 at Bootcamp has been an extremely valuable experience, where I have learned a lot of things, including holding numerous different roles in my groups and learning what they do and how they serve the team. My roles were vibes watcher, product owner, and git keeper.

Week 2: Vibes watcher
For my week 2 project I was incharge of the vibes! It was my job to make sure vibes were high, people were in a good space and people were happy and enjoying themselves. For this week my group only had 3 people so that made my role as vibe watcher easier to undertake because of having fewer people to look out for. All in all I think I performed well in this role, it taught me a lot about managing emotions and vibes in a team and taught me how important good team morale is for a project to be completed to a high quality standard.

Week 4: Product owner
For my week 4 project I was the product owner. Typically the product owner is the member of the team that has direct contact with the client and then relays what the client wants to the scrub facilitator, who then relays that back to the remaining team. Being product owner in this group project taught me a few things about the role, especially stuff about group management (as the product owner also doubled as a bit of a group manager in this weeks group project). That said, I don’t feel like I learnt a huge amount about the role seeing how there was no actual client involved in the project.

Week 5: Git keeper
For my week 5 project I was the man who was in charge of the git. It was my job to decide the layout of my teams git flow (we decided to create our own branches, merge them with main once we were done, pull from main to ensure we had the most up to date version, then create a new branch and start the cycle again). I was also in charge of making sure all commits and merges were done safely and with no issues, and also in charge of sorting out any conflicts. I learned the most as my role of git keeper than any other role as we used git throughout the entire day of the project, and I definitely finished up the day feeling like I had a better understanding of git and git workflow than I had before I started the project.
