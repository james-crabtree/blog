HUMAN SKILLS REFLECTIONS: Personal and professional growth

In terms of learning, one thing I’ve learnt from doing foundations and bootcamp is that I am a very social person and that I learn a heck of a lot better when I am actually in person and working alongside someone else (like pair programming) or working alongside a whole group of people (like in group projects).


I have developed a whole range of useful human skills while on bootcamp too. These include active listening, having tough and sometimes uncomfortable conversations with peers, ways of dealing with stress, and ways of being respectful towards others as well as working on bias of others and inclusion of others. One thing I noticed a few weeks back was that when I met my friend for coffee, the human skills I had learnt just automatically and instinctively kicked in - I was maintaining eye contact, not interrupting when they were talking, and also reciprocating back what they were saying to me to show that I was listening and that I was understanding what they were saying.


Learning about the practice of self compassion has had a huge effect on not only my learning at bootcamp, but also just in my day to day life as well. In the past I have been very hard on myself if I wasn’t understanding a topic, or if I wasn’t getting the hang of something quickly. The human skills taught me that self compassion is essential for learning and that the whole process of learning is a journey in which everyone takes a different time. Because of this, I have been able to find a lot of calm when learning new things and I haven’t stressed out or worried myself if I wasn’t immediately understanding the topic.
