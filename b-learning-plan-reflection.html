HUMAN SKILLS REFLECTIONS: Learning plan reflections

Back in foundations I created a learning plan which I then revised at the start of bootcamp. Today we are nearing the end of bootcamp and I am here to reflect on my learning plan and see what worked well, what didn’t work well, and what I would potentially change for future learning plans.


Were there any surprises or did I learn anything new about myself?.
While it didn’t come as a massive surprise, I definitely learnt more about myself in the fact that I learn best when I am around other people, in person and working alongside other people face to face. This was something that I had known prior to starting bootcamp, but undergoing bootcamp kind of surprised me just how much better I work and learn when I’m surrounded by other people face to face, and when I get the chance to work alongside other people.


I suppose the burning question that everyone is waiting to be answered is, did I actually follow the stuff I laid out in my learning plan? One of my plans was to be a kind and just generally nice person to be around, not be a dickhead in other words, so that the campus environment is a good one. I certainly think I followed this, I’ve been kind and I think a good person to be around, though admittedly there was 1 or 2 times I may have accidentally said something dickheadish - but I can live with that.

I also made a commitment to get help after 10-15 minutes of being stuck on a problem, whether that is help from Google, ChatGPT, a fellow student, or a facilitator. I actually think I stuck to this learning commitment really well, there were even a few times where I would instantly seek help (instead of struggling for 10 minutes) just because I was aware of my limitations and that the problem I was facing was something I couldn’t fix on my own at that point.

I also made a commitment to take a step back and relax when stuff was getting tough, or when things were getting to me. This is something I followed very well, in fact sometimes maybe there were times where I followed it to well and felt a lot more relaxed than I should’ve been, or taken a bigger step back from something that was hard than I really should of.
